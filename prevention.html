Prevention

The majority of skin cancer occur due to overexposure to ultraviolet rays. UV rays come from the sun, tanning beds, and sunlamps. The easiest way to protect yourself from skin cancer is protecting your skin from UV rays from the sun and other artificial sources.

Protection from UV rays is necessary all year, not just during hot days. Even during cloudy days, you will be exposed to UV. You can check the UV Index to see the strength of UV rays each day, if it is higher than 3, you need to protect your skin from exposure through methods such as:

  • Staying in the shade
  • Wear clothing that covers your arms and legs
  • Wear a hat with a wide brim to shade your face, head, ears, and neck
  • Wear sunglasses that block both UVA and UVB
  • Use a broad spectrum sunscreen with a sun protection factor (SPF) of 15 or higher
